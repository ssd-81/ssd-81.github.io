I have recently been focusing on problem solving. Recently I have been trying to solve problems on binary search; the topic has been pretty familiar and I had studied it in my undergrad Data Structures class as well. But, when I started to work on few standard problems (which build on top of each other), I noticed a few gaps in my understanding. The problems I used were:
1. Search Insert Position
2. First and Last Occurence of a number
3. Cutting Wood
I was able to get through the problem by managing to write a solution (which was produced after multiple iterations of failed cases). But when I revisited some of these problems, and the ideas were not as clear in mind, I came to a realization that I don't really understand them as well as thought. 

--- 
I sat with the confusion for long enough and forced myself to come up with half baked ideas; the process was surely not entertaining or enjoyable (I guess the point of really learning something is not as enjoyable as we think). But after some serious struggle and thought, I came to an understanding around some deeper ideas on binary search like upper bound, lower bound, edge cases, minicule optimizations that might not even make sense. This whole struggle , though made me realize that sitting with resistance rather than simply trying to move onto the next bandwagon can bring serious change to how we approach problems. 

---
This was a reflection on how I was approaching problems and how this exercise has helped me with sitting with ambiguity.
